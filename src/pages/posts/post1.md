# PSA: If you live in Canada you should know this about Kindle

The Kindle is not a bad device by any means. In fact I still use my 10 year old Kindle Paperwhite. It's more scratched up than before and the battery life is worse, but I think it still has another few years of life.

However there is one major flaw with Kindles that Canadians should be aware of, and that's access to library books.

That's right. **Kindle ereaders can't read ebooks from Canadian libraries**. In the US, Amazon and Overdrive (owner of Libby) have an agreement that allows you to send your borrowed ebooks directly to Kindle devices from the Libby app. I used this feature a lot while living in the US. I was quite surprised find this functionality missing after I moved back to Canada.

Canadian libraries are generally aware of this and recommend patrons to use other ereader devices [1,2]. Practically any other device you buy, including Kindle tablets which run Android, will be able to read library books:

## Technically...
Ok technically what I said is incorrect. Kindles ereaders _can_ open ebooks (epubs) that don't have DRM. I've noticed this with Brandon Sanderson's books. When you borrow them on Libby you still can't send them directly to Kindle from the Libby app like you can in the US, but you _can_ download the epub which is offered by the publisher without DRM and then use Amazon's [send to kindle](https://www.amazon.ca/sendtokindle) feature to add it to your Kindle library.

It turns out this is because Tor as a publisher offers all its books without DRM [3]. In fact in the last 20 years from 2006-2025, 9/20 Hugo award winners for Best Novel were from Tor Publishing. So if you're reading mostly from Tor and other non-DRM publishers, maybe you can get away with a Kindle.

## Closing remarks
The Kindle will be the best device for reading Kindle books. By far it will be the most integrated with Amazon's ecosystem which would be especially important if you have a large library of Kindle ebooks already.

However if
1. the above doesn't sound like you
2. you live in Canada and value reading ebooks from your local library
3. you want an e-ink device
then I believe a Kobo or a newer Android-based device like Boox is your best option.
